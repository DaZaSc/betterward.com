Retrospective Benchmarks for Machine Intelligence

According to historical definitions of intelligence, mind, or thought—have we built it?

Each historical thinker who defined "intelligence" or "mind" or "soul" left us something like a specification. This project treats those texts as "unwitting benchmarks"—testable specifications their authors never intended as such. We operationalize the specification into criteria, evaluate current AI systems against those criteria, and report results.

The findings: scores range from 32% to 100% depending on which definition is used. That spread is not measurement error—it is conceptual disagreement made visible.

The Method, Demonstrated

Plato's Academy defined man as a ζῷον δίπουν ἄπτερον—a featherless biped. Diogenes refuted it with a plucked chicken. We apply the same method to AI:

Criterion Score
Featherless (ἄπτερον) 100%
Biped (δίπουν) 0%
Overall 50%

By the Platonic definition, current AI is half a man.
