Retrospective Benchmarks for Machine Intelligence

According to historical definitions of Artificial General Intelligence (AGI), have we achieved it?

Six definitions, spanning 1997 to 2023, each treated as a testable specification. Each asked a different question about what machine intelligence would require. Each yields a different verdict when applied to frontier AI systems as of December 2025.

Chapter 1: The Gubrud Benchmark (1997) Does it rival the human brain in complexity and speed?
Chapter 2: The Reinvention Benchmark (2002) Can a single system handle broad cognitive tasks?
Chapter 3: The Formalization Benchmark (2007) Does it achieve goals and learn across environments?
Chapter 4: The Corporatization Benchmark (2018) Can it autonomously outperform humans at economically valuable work?
Chapter 5: The Critique Benchmark (2019) Does it efficiently acquire skills on genuinely novel tasks?
Chapter 6: The Synthesis Benchmark (2023) Where does it fall on the spectrum from emerging to superhuman?
